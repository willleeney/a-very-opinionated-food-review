Review Redesign

Three approaches for adding dish-level reviews, photos, and filtering — while keeping the editorial warmth of the existing design.

A — Dish Cards (Expanded Row) B — Photo Strip (Expanded Row) C — Detail Popup (Modal)

Mockup A

Dish Cards

Reviews are grouped by dish within the expanded table row. Each review can optionally name a dish and attach a photo. Photos appear as small thumbnails inline with the review. Dish names become filterable chips at the top of the expanded section.

Dish filter chips appear at the top of the expanded row. "All" is default. Clicking a dish name filters reviews to just that dish. "Overall" shows reviews with no dish specified.

Photos sit as 48px thumbnails on the left of each review. Reviews without photos are indented to align. Clicking a thumbnail opens a lightbox.

Dish Input

Free-text input with autocomplete from previously entered dishes for the same restaurant. "Leave blank for overall review" hint keeps it optional and low-friction.

Photo Storage

Photos uploaded to Supabase Storage bucket. Stored as reviews/{review_id}.jpg. Thumbnails generated on upload (64px, 200px). Original kept for lightbox.

Filtering

Dish chips are derived from reviews — no separate table. Filter is local to the expanded row. Could extend to the global filter bar as a "dish" dropdown in future.

Mockup B

Photo Strip

The expanded row leads with a horizontal photo strip — all dish photos from every review, scrollable. Below it, reviews are listed in the existing format with a dish label badge. The add-review form is a taller modal with a prominent photo area and a dish field grouped with rating.

Photo strip sits above the reviews — a scrollable row of 64px thumbnails. Clicking one opens it in a larger lightbox view. Active photo gets an accent border.

Dish filter is a minimal <select> dropdown instead of chips — takes less space and works better when there are many dishes. "All reviews" is default.

Photo-First

Large upload zone in the form encourages photos. The strip in the expanded row creates a visual "gallery" feel even with just a few photos.

Grouped Fields

Dish and Rating sit on the same row — you're describing what you ate and how you rated it in one glance. Reduces form height.

Dropdown Filter

A <select> dropdown is compact and fits the existing filter bar style. Scales to any number of dishes without layout issues.

Mockup C

Detail Popup

Instead of expanding a row, clicking a restaurant opens a centered popup (modal) with a photo grid at the top and reviews below. The add-review form is inside this popup as a collapsible section. This gives more room for photos and richer layout.

Photo grid sits at the top of the popup like a hero image. The grid layout adapts: 1 photo fills the width, 2 photos split 50/50, 3+ shows a "+N" overlay on the last slot. Clicking opens a full-screen lightbox.

Dish tabs use a horizontal tab bar (underline style) — feels more like navigation within the popup. "All" is active by default. Tabs are derived from review dish names.

Inline form at the bottom is collapsible — "Add your review" header toggles it open. Photo upload is a compact 100px square to the left of the fields, keeping everything on one "row".

Modal vs Row

Replaces the expanded row pattern. Gives much more room for photos and reviews. The table row click now opens this popup instead of expanding inline.

Tab Navigation

Underline tabs feel like browsing a menu. The "Overall" tab catches reviews that didn't specify a dish. Could add a "Photos" tab that shows only the gallery.

Collapsible Form

Form defaults to collapsed so the focus is on reading reviews. Opens when you want to add yours. Keeps the popup clean when you're just browsing.
